The lesson is the loop.

Loop Engineering is an emerging practice: define the goal, take a bounded action, observe external evidence, adjust from causality, then pass, stop, roll back, or escalate. Those loops live inside every SDLC phase.

Read the learning-loop diagram as text
  1. Goal: define outcome, proof, constraints, budget, authority, and exit.
  2. Act: make the smallest safe, reversible change.
  3. Observe: inspect tests, traces, users, telemetry, or review.
  4. Adjust: change the plan or implementation from that evidence.
  5. Gate: pass, repeat, roll back, stop, or escalate.

The loop can run inside conceive/plan, specify, design, build/integrate, verify/validate, release/operate, and maintain/retire.

Read the three-minute arc as text
  1. 0:00–0:20: see a deterministic failure and a visible goal.
  2. 0:20–0:45: make one scoped change and earn a first result.
  3. 0:45–1:30: read a partial pass and the actual mismatch.
  4. 1:30–2:20: catch regression and adjust without weakening criteria.
  5. 2:20–3:00: pass every gate, release or escalate, and invite replay.

Stormglass

Rotate the prisms of a sky-lighthouse, run repeatable storm tests, and use lightning traces to open one verified route for a courier airship.

Why it is distinct: evidence fills the sky. The player calibrates one optical variable, compares route traces, and decides between a fast fragile path and a longer robust path.

Core verbAlign prisms
EvidenceRoute traces
EmotionUrgency → relief

The three-minute loop

0:00–0:20

Baseline: pull the lever; crosswind collapses the courier route.

0:20–0:45

First success: rotate one prism; the nearest cloud ring clears.

0:45–1:30

New evidence: the fast route overloads the lighthouse.

1:30–2:20

Adjustment: compare ghost paths and preserve both acceptance criteria.

2:20–3:00

Release: pass a repeat test and commit the safe corridor.

Build and judge fit

Controls
Drag-and-snap or select-to-cycle through three discrete orientations; one test/commit control.
Four-hour floor
Two required prisms, three authored paths, two criteria, one airship animation, no volumetric weather.
Free assets
Quaternius Ships, Fantasy Props, modular ruins, or primitive silhouettes.
Win story
The clearest cinematic before/after transformation.
Risk and cut
If VFX is unreadable after 30 minutes, use ribbon paths over layered cloud cards.

The Tomorrow Garden

Run accelerated seasons in a seedship terrarium, compare the evidence left by each cycle, and cultivate a habitat that can sustain itself after release.

Why it is distinct: the system is grown rather than assembled. Time, dependencies, and stability become visible plant silhouettes instead of charts.

Core verbCultivate
EvidenceSeason overlays
EmotionCare → wonder

The three-minute loop

0:00–0:20

Baseline: a sprout grows, but the habitat cannot reproduce.

0:20–0:45

First success: add one sun tile and rerun the season.

0:45–1:30

Regression: extra water helps one plant and floods another.

1:30–2:20

Adjustment: compare prior gardens, prune one branch, rebalance one input.

2:20–3:00

Release: a full unattended season passes and the ark pod seals.

Build and judge fit

Controls

Four-hour floor

Free assets

Win story
An unusually gentle and memorable explanation of stability and dependency.

Kaiju QA

Before a helpful baby kaiju meets a real city, build a tabletop test district, stage edge cases, and prove that its behavior is safe enough to ship.

Why it is distinct: the player is not the monster or its programmer. They are the quality engineer. Test coverage becomes a miniature city and over-broad fixes create funny, visible regression.

Core verbStage tests
EvidenceCoverage paths
EmotionComedy → confidence

The three-minute loop

0:00–0:20

Happy path: the kaiju carries a stalled car and earns a partial pass.

0:20–0:45

Edge case: place a fragile tower; helpful behavior knocks it over.

0:45–1:30

Regression: a broad freeze rule protects the tower but blocks an ambulance.

1:30–2:20

Adjustment: compare both paths and place a targeted slow-zone boundary.

2:20–3:00

Release: every scenario passes; the tiny kaiju becomes the city guardian.

Build and judge fit

Controls
Drag one scenario prop or boundary into a socket; inspect a path; press Run.
Four-hour floor
Four buildings, one creature, two props, three authored paths, one scale-up finale.
Free assets
Quaternius Cute/Ultimate Monsters, Simple Buildings, Cars, Animated Robot.
Win story
Fastest comprehension, clearest regression, strongest screenshot and audience reaction.
Risk and cut
No destruction physics. Every outcome is an authored, reversible state transition.

Sunrise Express

Rehearse a magical release train across an orbital switchyard, diagnose where dependencies stop, and dispatch a verified sunrise to the city below.

Why it is distinct: the train is the release artifact, not a logistics economy. The player sees integration order, quality gates, rollback, and operation as one civic ritual.

Core verbRoute rehearsal
EvidenceGhost trains
EmotionMomentum → triumph

The three-minute loop

0:00–0:20

Baseline: one light car arrives, but the full train stops at a dependency.

0:20–0:45

First success: flip one switch; two cars pass the next gate.

0:45–1:30

Quality tradeoff: a shortcut is fast but leaves one car unverified.

1:30–2:20

Adjustment: use the suspended route trace to choose a robust path.

2:20–3:00

Release: dispatch the train; its wake lights the city and monitor.

Build and judge fit

Controls

Four-hour floor

Restore an unfinished kinetic exhibit by comparing layered prototypes, finding their shared mismatch, and preserving every useful failure in the final verified story.

Why it is distinct: the evidence itself is the toy. An inspection lens, translucent attempts, and one test crank turn diffs, regression protection, and retrospectives into spatial play.

Core verbInspect and compare
EvidencePrototype layers
EmotionMystery → wonder

0:20–0:45

First success: align an expected silhouette and replace one worn part.

0:45–1:30

New evidence: the wing passes, but flight still fails.

1:30–2:20

Adjustment: overlay attempts and fix the cause without erasing prior gains.

2:20–3:00

Release: the bird flies and the exhibit preserves the complete attempt history.

Build and judge fit
