Loop Engineer: Kaiju QA

Test the tiny monster before it meets the real city.

Before a helpful baby kaiju is deployed to a real city, build a tabletop test
district, stage edge cases, and prove that its "help everyone" behavior is safe
enough to ship.

The player is the quality engineer. A happy-path stalled-car test passes, but a
fragile-tower edge case fails. A broad "freeze near buildings" guardrail saves
the tower and causes a new ambulance regression. The player compares persistent
path evidence, preserves both criteria, places a targeted slow-zone boundary,
runs the full regression suite, and releases only after every scenario passes.

Loop Engineering means defining the goal, authorizing a bounded action,
observing external evidence, adjusting from causality, and reaching an explicit
pass, stop, rollback, or escalate gate. In this game, those decisions become
spatial toys instead of a static diagram.

Audience: first-year computing students, bootcamp learners, career switchers,
and educators teaching responsible AI-assisted development.

Concept visualization. Final submission media should replace mock scenes with
matching gameplay capture when available.
