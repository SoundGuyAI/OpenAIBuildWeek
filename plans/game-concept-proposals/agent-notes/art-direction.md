Role: art director and information-design lead
Scope: visual proposal architecture for 20 cross-platform WebXR concepts

employs an NPC mentor who teaches agentic software development across the
software development life cycle (SDLC) and SDLR through loop engineering.

## Visual thesis

The proposal should work like a wall of movie posters crossed with a transit
map. A reader must understand each concept's fantasy, dominant verb, spatial
shape, platform fit, and risk before reading a paragraph. Body copy confirms
what the image has already communicated.

Use one strong silhouette per concept, one unmistakable player action, and one
visible cause-and-effect transformation. Avoid generic headset imagery,
floating HUD rectangles, logo-like compositions, and scenes where all concepts
look like variants of neon sci-fi.

## Page architecture

Target a short, landscape proposal of **8–10 pages**. A 16:9 layout also makes
pages useful as presentation slides and browser-native review artifacts.

1. **Cover / promise** — one full-bleed four-world hero montage, a six-word
   proposition, and three platform pictograms. No concept descriptions.
2. **How to read the proposal** — universal loop infographic, input mapping,
   color legend, and five scope/risk symbols.
3. **Twenty-concept atlas** — all concepts as thumbnail cards in a 5 × 4 grid.
   Every card shows art, number, name, verb icon, mode, and risk pip.
4. **Concept spreads A–F** — each spread features one large cluster artwork and
   three or four compact concept cards. Use different panel geometry on each
   spread so radically different fantasies do not feel templated into sameness.
5. **Originality map** — plot all 20 concepts by player verb and world response;
   connect only intentional thematic neighbors.
6. **Scope / risk decision page** — matrix, shortlist funnel, and three recommended
   hackathon candidates with distinct audience promises.

Reading order should be image → name → verb → loop strip → one-line hook → body.
Never put more than 65 words of prose on a concept card.

## Color system

Use a dark neutral paper color (`#10141D`) and warm off-white (`#F4F0E6`) as the
two page grounds. Concept accents are functional, not decorative.

- **Cyan `#31D9E6` — systems / learning:** planning, orchestration, diagnosis.
- **Tangerine `#FF8A3D` — dexterity / timing:** cooking, catching, sequencing.
- **Jade `#38D996` — care / cooperation:** healing, growing, shared control.
- **Ultraviolet `#9A6CFF` — mystery / perception:** sound, shadows, deduction.
- **Magenta `#F257A6` — social / expressive:** negotiation, rumor, performance.
- **Cobalt `#478CFF` — traversal / world shaping:** routes, maps, gravity, tides.
- **Ochre `#D9A441` — history / construction:** archaeology, evidence, repair.

Every card receives one category color plus a concept-specific tint. Risk colors
must not rely on red/green alone: use shape and labels in the source document.
Check all text/background combinations to WCAG AA; generated artwork receives a
20–35% dark or light edge wash beneath overlaid type.

## Iconography

Build a repository-native, single-stroke SVG family using a 24 × 24 viewBox,
2 px round strokes, and no fills except state dots. Icons need to survive at
16 px and should describe verbs rather than genres.

- **Player verbs:** open hand/grab, rotating arrows/align, branching node/plan,
  needle/stitch, ear/listen, sprout/grow, prism/inspect, knot/coordinate.
- **World response:** bloom, fracture, reroute, reveal, rewind, harmonize.
- **Platforms:** mouse with drag arc; phone with thumb orbit; two controllers
  with pinch rays; optional hand-tracking palm.
- **Mode:** seated eye, room-scale boundary, shared link, solo figure.
- **Risk:** one to five nested diamonds; outline means unknown, filled means
  validated. Never use a flame, skull, or warning triangle as the only signal.

Each concept gets a two-glyph signature: **verb + response**. For example, Loop
Engineer is `branching node → harmonize`, while Gravity Tailor is `needle →
reroute`. This makes the atlas understandable even when names are hidden.

## Typographic hierarchy

Favor open-source variable fonts that render cleanly in browser and PDF.

- **Display:** Space Grotesk Variable, 48–72 pt, 600–700 weight, tight tracking.
- **Concept names:** Space Grotesk, 22–28 pt, 650 weight, one or two lines.
- **Hooks / pull quotes:** Fraunces Italic, 18–24 pt, used sparingly to give each
  fantasy a human voice.
- **Body / data:** Inter Variable, 10.5–13 pt, 450–600 weight.
- **Micro labels:** Inter, 9–10 pt, uppercase, +0.08 em tracking; never smaller.

Numbers `01–20` should be oversized translucent wayfinding marks, not badges.
Use sentence case everywhere else. Do not place paragraphs over busy art.

On the atlas page, collapse to a vertical 4:3 thumbnail.

│                                                          │

The generated art itself must contain no text or UI. The repository-native card
adds accessible labels, icons, captions, and alt text after generation.

### Loop Engineer card direction

Show the player at the near edge of a circular workbench and a friendly hired
NPC mentor opposite them. The workbench makes agentic software development
visible: one software task improves through the software development life cycle
(SDLC) and SDLR by repeated loop engineering. Repository-native labels identify
`SDLC`, `SDLR`, and `loop engineering` without turning either framework into an
acronym-derived stage mnemonic. Five tangible activities form a clockwise loop:

1. a pulsing observation lens reveals a tangled requirement or defect;
2. spatial plan and acceptance-check tiles assemble into a route;
3. tiny specialist agents launch toward bounded work areas;
4. a crystalline test gate changes from fractured to stable while evidence
   tokens collect beside it;
5. a reflection pool compares the result with the request and feeds the next
   improvement back to station one.

Use pose, gaze, color flow, and object transformation to communicate teaching:
the mentor demonstrates at one station while the player completes the next.
The loop should read clockwise at thumbnail size. Avoid classrooms, chalkboards,
code screens, text labels, corporate office imagery, or an all-knowing AI god.
The NPC is an employed collaborator and coach; the player retains agency.

## Mini-infographic patterns

- **Five-beat loop strip:** five equal pictograms linked by a single directional
  ribbon; the last arrow visibly feeds the first. Each beat shows a before/after
  state, not merely a noun.
- **Three-platform braid:** mouse, touch, and XR gestures enter from three lanes,
  converge on one game verb, then exit into the same world response.
- **Fantasy / verb / payoff triplet:** three large symbols read left-to-right;
  e.g. `mentor + delegate + stable machine`.
- **Ten-second demo:** a three-frame storyboard labeled in the source layout as
  setup, delightful action, and shareable reveal.
- **Comfort capsule:** horizon lock, seated support, reach envelope, motion type,
  and session length represented by five small measurable gauges.
- **Risk stack:** technology, content, interaction, comfort, and schedule shown
  as separate diamond rows, preventing one vague “difficulty” score.

## Repository-native SVG infographic designs

All infographics should be authored as semantic SVG with a `viewBox`, `<title>`,
`<desc>`, grouped layers, CSS custom properties, and adjacent HTML/Markdown alt
text. Keep live text as SVG `<text>` or document text; never rasterize labels.

### 1. Universal game loop — “The responsive orbit”

- **Canvas:** 1200 × 560.
- **Form:** five large nodes on an open elliptical orbit: perceive, choose, act,
  world responds, learn. A thick tapered ribbon returns learn to perceive.
- **Second layer:** three smaller colored satellites under “act” show mouse,
  touch, and XR gestures converging on the same verb icon.
- **Proof of fun:** center of the orbit holds a before/after world silhouette.
- **Loop Engineer variant:** place `software development life cycle (SDLC)` and
  `SDLR` as parallel framework labels above a ribbon titled `loop engineering`.
  Keep the activity nodes as concrete verbs—understand, plan, delegate, test,
  inspect evidence, improve—rather than mapping letters to stages. Place mentor
  and player silhouettes on alternating nodes to show guided transfer of agency.

### 2. Platform input mappings — “Three hands, one consequence”

- **Canvas:** 1440 × 720.
- **Form:** Sankey-like braid from three input lanes into six universal verbs,
  then into shared world responses.
- **Desktop lane:** point, click/hold, drag, wheel, keys.
- **Mobile lane:** tap/hold, one-finger drag, two-finger orbit, pinch, tilt only
  when optional.
- **XR lane:** ray select, direct grab, two-hand scale/rotate, thumbstick, hand
  pinch only when tracked.
- Lines share the concept accent after convergence, emphasizing feature parity
  rather than portraying desktop/mobile as degraded modes.

### 3. Originality map — “Verb × world response constellation”

- **Canvas:** 1200 × 900.
- **Axes:** horizontal from precise/local action to systemic/world action;
  vertical from puzzle certainty to expressive emergence.
- Plot concepts as numbered verb-response glyphs, not title bubbles. Use faint
  category-color territories rather than quadrants.
- Add a thin ring around concepts with a strong market comparable and a dashed
  ring around concepts whose combination appears less familiar. Caption this
  as differentiation evidence, never proof of absolute originality.
- Keep Loop Engineer's SDLC/SDLR loop-engineering model near systemic/certain
  but offset toward emergence because delegation outcomes, tests, evidence, and
  reflection change the next software plan.

### 4. Scope / risk matrix — “Hackathon weather chart”

- **Canvas:** 1200 × 800.
- **Axes:** implementation days on x; integration uncertainty on y.
- Bubble size = bespoke content burden. Inner icon = highest risk category:
  interaction, XR comfort, networking, simulation, art, or learning design.
- A translucent 2-day “buildable front” curves through the lower-left, avoiding
  a simplistic square cutoff. Candidate bubbles include a tiny fallback wedge
  showing the minimum lovable slice.

### 5. Shortlist funnel — “From twenty worlds to one prototype”

- **Canvas:** 1440 × 650.
- **Form:** not a corporate funnel. Use four nested stage apertures cut into a
  theatrical set: 20 territories → 8 distinct demos → 3 feasibility prototypes
  → 1 audience-tested build.
- Each aperture filters by visible tokens: distinct verb, ten-second delight,
  cross-platform parity, XR comfort, two-day scope, and shareable reveal.
- Rejected concepts remain visible on side shelves with a reason glyph, signaling
  a reusable idea library rather than “losers.”

## Image selection and QA

For each generated plate, request 3–4 seeded variants and select for action
legibility, not surface beauty. Review at three sizes: full page, 25% zoom, and
160 px atlas thumbnail. Reject any image if a reviewer cannot identify the actor,
action, target, and resulting change in three seconds.

Before integration:

- crop safely to 16:9 and a 4:3 card derivative without losing the main action;
- export display assets as high-quality WebP, retaining a lossless source only
  when future edits justify it;
- add descriptive alt text that names the fantasy and visible interaction;
- verify there is no accidental pseudo-text, watermark, logo, or malformed hand;
- run contrast checks after overlays and inspect PDF rendering at 100%;
- use Git LFS only for retained large lossless masters, not normal WebP outputs.

The final visual test is simple: hide every paragraph and title except concept
names. If the twenty cards still look like twenty different games—and Loop
Engineer unmistakably reads as learning agent orchestration from a mentor—the
system is working.
